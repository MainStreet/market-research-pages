1. Another Conversation with Val Bercovici Memory Markets

This podcast discussion between Doug O'Laughlin and Val Bercovici from WEKA explores the rapidly evolving memory market for AI inference. The speakers examine how KV cache offloading has transitioned from a confusing concept to essential infrastructure as agentic AI demands surge.

The conversation highlights that AI startups now consume trillion-token consumption as a routine thing, daily sometimes. They discuss Jensen Huang's endorsement of context memory storage extending beyond HBM and DRAM to NVMe, which validates the growing market.

Key topics include the distinction between logical and physical KV caching, pricing arbitrage opportunities (with cached reads at roughly 10% of list price), and infrastructure constraints. The speakers note that current cache systems only support up to one-hour retention windows, indicating DRAM limitations without NVMe offloading.

They analyze network architecture challenges, comparing CXL's resurrection with NVIDIA's three-network approach, and discuss DeepSeek's approach of dynamically allocating network resources rather than hard segregation.

The discussion concludes that memory infrastructure will remain critical as long as transformer models dominate, potentially lasting a long, long time in the AI landscape.
