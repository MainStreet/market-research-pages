CVX — Chevron Corporation

Chevron reported first-quarter earnings that significantly beat Street expectations, with EPS of $1.41 versus the consensus of $0.95. The company benefited from record U.S. production exceeding 2 million barrels per day for the third consecutive quarter and record refinery throughput in March. Despite Middle East supply disruptions from the Strait of Hormuz closure, Chevron's diversified global portfolio and less than 5% Middle East exposure limited impact.

Key points

  • Third consecutive quarter of U.S. production greater than 2 million barrels per day
  • U.S. production increased 500,000 barrels per day year-over-year, from 3.5 million to 4.4 million barrels per day globally
  • Record U.S. refinery throughput achieved in March
  • Less than 5% of production exposed to Middle East

XOM — ExxonMobil Corporation

ExxonMobil reported first-quarter results that beat expectations, crediting its long-term diversified portfolio strategy rather than attempting to predict market conditions. The company highlighted more than doubling U.S. production since 2018 at over 10% compounded annual growth rate, a successful 200,000 barrel-per-day refining investment that has already paid for itself, and the recent startup of its Gulf Coast LNG plant increasing U.S. LNG exports by 5%. Despite 20% of total production being affected by Strait of Hormuz closure, only 3% of facilities were directly impacted.

Key points

  • Beat first-quarter earnings expectations

  • Gulf Coast LNG plant startup increases U.S. LNG exports by approximately 5%
  • 20% of total production blocked by Strait of Hormuz closure, but only 3% of facilities impacted
  • Diversified global portfolio reduces Middle East exposure
